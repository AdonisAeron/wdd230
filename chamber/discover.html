A well maintained economy

Our presence in the industrial age began in the early 20th century with textiles as led us to our current standing as a automotovie manufacturing leader, we have taken contorl of our industrial strengths and became a high-growth economic hotseat. Spartanburg County grew from a frontier to a textile hub into our current well diversified, manufacturing heart. Spartanburg houses multiple world-class international firms and the highest per-capita foreign investment in the entierty of South Carolina.

A prime location

With a rich history of logistics and a geographic location right ontop of the intersection of interstates 85 and 26, Spartanburg could be called nothing but well positioned. Spartanburg posses low effort access to Greenville-Spartanburg international Airport, the SC Inland Port - Greer, and the Port of Charelston. Spartanburg Country offers all of our businesses a beatiful transportation system, boasting highways, railways, and truck lines!

Good company

Spartanburg county is a hub for world class international businesses and firms. Spartanburg hosts a diverse economy consisting of multiple large industrial Fortune 500 companies. This includes companies such as BMW, Milliken, Michelin, adidas, Dräxlmaier, Toray Carbon Fibers, Röchling, Magna, Auriga Polymers, Advance America, Denny's, Kobelco and many many more. Automotive, advanced materials, distribution, energy, biosciences and aerospace industries can all be found inside of our humble country and are all thriving and expected to continue doing so.

With a successful history

In 2019, we hosted 27 economic projects totaling more than $354 million in capital investment, creating over 811 new jobs for our locals. Over the past 5 years, we have managed 100 projects totaling more than $3 blilion in capital investment, this alone created 7,993 jobs. We have been named in Site Selection Magazine's "Best to Invest Rankings" three times.
